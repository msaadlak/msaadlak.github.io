An Ontology for Evolving Food Recipes

Recipes have several characteristics and unique factors (such as ingredients, actions, nutritional values, cooking time, meal type, and many more). These factors have changed the perspective of recipes from just a taste desire to a trade-off between taste requirements and nutritional needs. A diabetic person might be interested in sweet recipes but with less calories. Similarly a diet conscious person might be interested in recipes with high protien but less fat saturation. Nevertheless some food enthusiasts might have constraints while searching recipes (e.g. find recipes that can be made using chicken and oven). High level description of recipes may better help to semantically understand the culinary process. Such semantic rich descriptions not only increase the interoperability but also helps to improve the machine readability of data. Enormous recipe related data is available on numerous websites. Heterogeneity of data on these websites is a major hurdle to automate the recipe extraction process and creation of semantic rich descriptions. In this article we have proposed EvoRecipes that is an effective ontological description to semantically understand the recipe creation and recipe evolution process. Moreover we have followed a modular approach to create EvoRecipes.
